# Daily AI Papers Digest — April 20, 2026

## Overview

Today's digest covers 20 recent papers from arXiv spanning computer vision (cs.CV), artificial intelligence (cs.AI), machine learning (cs.LG), and computational linguistics (cs.CL). Key themes include advances in multimodal learning, LLM evaluation and reasoning, autonomous driving, 3D scene understanding, and novel training/optimization methods.

---

## Papers

### 1. Bidirectional Cross-Modal Prompting for Event-Frame Asymmetric Stereo
**Authors:** Ninghui Xu, Fabio Tosi, Lihui Wang, Jiawei Han, Luca Bartolomei et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15312v1

Addresses the modality gap between event cameras and conventional frame-based cameras for reliable 3D perception under fast motion and challenging illumination conditions using bidirectional cross-modal prompting.

---

### 2. LeapAlign: Post-Training Flow Matching Models at Any Generation Step by Building Two-Step Trajectories
**Authors:** Zhanhao Liang, Tao Yang, Jie Wu, Chengjian Feng, Liang Zheng et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15311v1

Proposes LeapAlign for aligning flow matching models with human preferences by fine-tuning through direct reward gradient backpropagation, addressing memory costs and gradient explosion in long trajectories.

---

### 3. TokenLight: Precise Lighting Control in Images using Attribute Tokens
**Authors:** Sumit Chaturvedi, Yannick Hold-Geoffroy, Mengwei Ren, Jingyuan Liu, He Zhang et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV, cs.GR
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15310v1

Introduces attribute tokens for precise, continuous control over multiple illumination attributes (intensity, color, ambient, diffuse, 3D position) in image relighting via conditional generation.

---

### 4. MM-WebAgent: A Hierarchical Multimodal Web Agent for Webpage Generation
**Authors:** Yan Li, Zezi Zeng, Yifan Yang, Yuqing Yang, Ning Liao et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV, cs.AI, cs.CL
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15309v1

Proposes a hierarchical agentic framework for multimodal webpage generation that integrates AIGC tools while maintaining style consistency and global coherence.

---

### 5. RAD-2: Scaling Reinforcement Learning in a Generator-Discriminator Framework
**Authors:** Hao Gao, Shaoyu Chen, Yifan Zhu, Yuehao Song, Wenyu Liu et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15308v1

A unified generator-discriminator framework for closed-loop autonomous driving planning that addresses stochastic instabilities in diffusion-based planners through reinforcement learning.

---

### 6. Generalization in LLM Problem Solving: The Case of the Shortest Path
**Authors:** Yao Tong, Jiayuan Ye, Anastasia Borovykh, Reza Shokri
**Categories:** cs.AI, cs.LG
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15306v1

Introduces a controlled synthetic environment based on shortest-path planning to study systematic generalization in language models, enabling clean separation of training data, paradigms, and inference strategies.

---

### 7. Diagnosing LLM Judge Reliability: Conformal Prediction Sets and Transitivity Violations
**Authors:** Manan Gupta, Dhruv Kumar
**Categories:** cs.AI, cs.CL, cs.LG
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15302v1

Presents a diagnostic toolkit for LLM-as-judge frameworks revealing widespread per-input inconsistency, with 33-67% of documents exhibiting transitivity violations masked by low aggregate rates.

---

### 8. Think in Latent Thoughts: A New Paradigm for Gloss-Free Sign Language Translation
**Authors:** Yiyang Jiang, Li Zhang, Xiao-Yong Wei, Li Qing
**Categories:** cs.CV
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15301v1

Reframes sign language translation as a cross-modal reasoning task using ordered latent thoughts as an explicit middle layer between video and generated text.

---

### 9. AnimationBench: Are Video Models Good at Character-Centric Animation?
**Authors:** Leyi Wu, Pengjun Fang, Kai Sun, Yazhou Xing, Yinwei Wu et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15299v1

Introduces a benchmark for evaluating animation-style video generation with focus on stylized appearance, exaggerated motion, and character-centric consistency.

---

### 10. Benchmarking Optimizers for MLPs in Tabular Deep Learning
**Authors:** Yury Gorishniy, Ivan Rubachev, Dmitrii Feoktistov, Artem Babenko
**Categories:** cs.LG
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15297v1

Systematic benchmark of optimizers for training MLP-based models on tabular data, examining alternatives to AdamW across multiple datasets.

---

### 11. How Do LLMs and VLMs Understand Viewpoint Rotation Without Vision?
**Authors:** Zhen Yang, Ping Jian, Zhongbin Guo, Zuming Zhang, Chengzhi Li
**Categories:** cs.AI
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15294v1

Investigates whether linguistic intelligence alone is sufficient for spatial intelligence, focusing on viewpoint rotation tasks with text-only inputs.

---

### 12. AD4AD: Benchmarking Visual Anomaly Detection Models for Safer Autonomous Driving
**Authors:** Fabrizio Genilotti, Arianna Stropeni, Gionata Grotto, Francesco Borsatti, Manuel Barusco et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV, cs.AI
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15291v1

Explores visual anomaly detection for autonomous driving to handle out-of-distribution conditions that degrade perception systems.

---

### 13. Structural Interpretability in SVMs with Truncated Orthogonal Polynomial Kernels
**Authors:** Víctor Soto-Larrosa, Nuria Torrado, Edmundo J. Huertas
**Categories:** stat.ML, cs.LG, math.ST
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15285v1

Introduces ORCA (Orthogonal Representation Contribution Analysis) for post-training interpretability of SVMs using truncated orthogonal polynomial kernels.

---

### 14. GlobalSplat: Efficient Feed-Forward 3D Gaussian Splatting via Global Scene Tokens
**Authors:** Roni Itkin, Noam Issachar, Yehonatan Keypur, Anpei Chen et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15284v1

Addresses spatial primitive allocation in 3D Gaussian Splatting using global scene tokens for better compactness, speed, and fidelity trade-offs.

---

### 15. R3D: Revisiting 3D Policy Learning
**Authors:** Zhengdong Hong, Shenrui Wu, Haozhe Cui, Boyi Zhao, Ran Ji et al.
**Categories:** cs.CV, cs.RO
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15281v1

Diagnoses training instabilities in 3D policy learning, identifying missing data augmentation and Batch Normalization issues, and proposes a transformer-based 3D encoder with diffusion decoder.

---

### 16. Why Do Vision Language Models Struggle To Recognize Human Emotions?

**Categories:** cs.CV, cs.AI
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15280v1

Investigates why even sophisticated VLMs fail to outperform specialized vision-only classifiers at recognizing human emotions.

---

### 17. How Embeddings Shape Graph Neural Networks: Classical vs Quantum-Oriented Node Representations
**Authors:** Nouhaila Innan, Antonello Rosato, Alberto Marchisio, Muhammad Shafique
**Categories:** cs.LG, quant-ph
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15273v1

Controlled benchmark comparing classical and quantum-oriented node embeddings for graph classification under a unified pipeline.

---

### 18. Prism: Symbolic Superoptimization of Tensor Programs
**Authors:** Mengdi Wu, Xiaoyu Jiang, Oded Padon, Zhihao Jia
**Categories:** cs.PL, cs.AI, cs.LG
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15272v1

First symbolic superoptimizer for tensor programs using sGraph, a hierarchical representation enabling two-level search over symbolic graphs and concrete implementations.

---

### 19. SegWithU: Uncertainty as Perturbation Energy for Medical Image Segmentation

**Categories:** cs.CV, cs.AI, cs.LG

Post-hoc framework augmenting frozen segmentation backbones with lightweight uncertainty heads for single-forward-pass risk-aware medical image segmentation.

---

### 20. Cloning is as Hard as Learning for Stabilizer States
**Authors:** Nikhil Bansal, Matthias C. Caro, Gaurav Mahajan
**Categories:** quant-ph, cs.LG, math.ST
