# AI Papers Digest — 2026-04-20
Selected recent arXiv papers.

## Overview
Recent arXiv activity shows a strong mix of work on reasoning quality, trustworthy evaluation, and agentic systems, alongside continued pressure to make multimodal and long-horizon models more useful in practice. Several papers focus less on raw benchmark gains and more on whether models reason faithfully, preserve diversity after post-training, or can be audited when embedded in research and production workflows. The overall trend is a maturing field that is increasingly concerned with reliability, controllability, and operational usefulness rather than capability in isolation.

## Papers
### 1. ASMR-Bench: Auditing for Sabotage in ML Research
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16286v1
- Summary: This paper introduces a benchmark for detecting subtle sabotage in ML research codebases, including changes to hyperparameters, data, and evaluation logic that can alter scientific conclusions without obvious red flags. It matters because autonomous research agents will only be useful if humans and models can reliably audit them, and the results here suggest that today’s best auditors still struggle on that task.

### 2. Learning to Reason with Insight for Informal Theorem Proving
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16278v1
- Summary: The authors argue that a key failure mode in informal theorem proving is not syntax but lack of strategic insight, and they address it with a hierarchical dataset plus progressive fine-tuning that explicitly teaches core techniques and proof sketches. The work is notable because it pushes reasoning training toward teaching models how to identify the right idea, not just how to continue a chain of text.

### 3. VEFX-Bench: A Holistic Benchmark for Generic Video Editing and Visual Effects
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16272v1
- Summary: VEFX-Bench introduces a human-annotated benchmark, dataset, and reward model for instruction-guided video editing, separating instruction following, rendering quality, and edit exclusivity. This matters because video generation is moving from novelty toward production tooling, and the paper offers a more realistic way to evaluate whether models can edit footage precisely rather than just generate visually plausible outputs.

### 4. Beyond Distribution Sharpening: The Importance of Task Rewards
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16259v1
- Summary: This paper directly tests a live debate in post-training: whether reinforcement learning creates genuinely new capabilities or simply sharpens a model’s existing output distribution. Its experiments argue that task-based rewards deliver more robust and stable gains than distribution sharpening alone, making it a useful contribution to how labs think about training reasoning models and agents.

### 5. Beyond Surface Statistics: Robust Conformal Prediction for LLMs via Internal Representations
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16217v1
- Summary: The paper proposes conformal prediction for LLM question answering using internal representation-based signals instead of fragile output-level confidence measures like entropy or token probabilities. That is important because reliable uncertainty estimates are one of the missing pieces for deploying LLMs in higher-stakes settings, especially under domain shift where surface confidence often breaks down.

### 6. JumpLoRA: Sparse Adapters for Continual Learning in Large Language Models
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16171v1
- Summary: JumpLoRA introduces sparsity-inducing gating in LoRA adapters to reduce interference between tasks during continual learning. The paper matters because practical LLM systems increasingly need to accumulate specialized capabilities over time without catastrophic forgetting, and adapter efficiency remains one of the most deployable paths to doing that.

### 7. AtManRL: Towards Faithful Reasoning via Differentiable Attention Saliency
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16158v1
- Summary: AtManRL combines outcome rewards with a saliency reward derived from differentiable attention manipulation so that chain-of-thought tokens that truly influence the answer are favored during training. The contribution is notable because it targets a central concern in reasoning research: getting models not just to produce persuasive traces, but to generate reasoning that is causally tied to correct answers.

### 8. Cut Your Losses! Learning to Prune Paths Early for Efficient Parallel Reasoning
- Date: 2026-04-17
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16029v1
- Summary: This work studies how to prune bad reasoning branches early in parallel reasoning systems and proposes STOP, a learnable internal pruning method that improves efficiency under fixed compute budgets. It matters because inference-time scaling is increasingly constrained by cost, and better branch selection could make large reasoning models materially cheaper and more practical to run.

## Themes
- Reliability is becoming a first-class research target, from sabotage auditing to calibrated uncertainty.
- Reasoning work is shifting toward faithfulness and insight, not just longer chains of thought.
- Post-training remains an active battleground, especially around what RL is actually adding to base models.
- Efficient deployment is a major theme, including continual learning and cheaper inference-time search.
- Multimodal evaluation is getting more realistic as video and editing systems move toward real workflows.
