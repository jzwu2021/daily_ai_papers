# AI Papers Digest — 2026-04-15

Selected recent arXiv papers.

## Overview
This paper set shows that the center of gravity in AI research remains post-training, reasoning quality, long-horizon agents, and multimodal reliability. Several of the most interesting papers focus less on simply scaling models and more on improving supervision, evaluation, inference efficiency, and hallucination control. Taken together, the trend is toward more usable, auditable, and operationally robust AI systems.

## Papers

### 1. Self-Distillation Zero: Self-Revision Turns Binary Rewards into Dense Supervision
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12002
- Summary: This paper proposes turning sparse binary rewards into denser self-supervision, which could make reasoning-oriented post-training substantially more sample-efficient in domains like math and code.

### 2. Nemotron 3 Super: Open, Efficient Mixture-of-Experts Hybrid Mamba-Transformer Model for Agentic Reasoning
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12374
- Summary: A notable open-model release combining MoE and hybrid sequence modeling ideas to target both stronger reasoning and more efficient long-context inference.

### 3. Relax: An Asynchronous Reinforcement Learning Engine for Omni-Modal Post-Training at Scale
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11554
- Summary: This systems paper matters because it tackles the practical infrastructure needed to scale RL post-training across text, image, audio, video, and agentic workloads.

### 4. Filtered Reasoning Score: Evaluating Reasoning Quality on a Model's Most-Confident Traces
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11996
- Summary: Better evaluation is becoming a bottleneck, and this work proposes a reasoning metric that looks beyond final-answer accuracy to the quality of confident reasoning traces.

### 5. The Long-Horizon Task Mirage? Diagnosing Where and Why Agentic Systems Break
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11978
- Summary: This is especially timely because long-horizon reliability remains one of the biggest unsolved problems in practical agent systems, and the paper aims to diagnose failure modes more precisely.

### 6. Benchmarking Deflection and Hallucination in Large Vision-Language Models
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12033
- Summary: The paper focuses on whether multimodal models can abstain appropriately instead of hallucinating, which is a key safety and product reliability requirement.

### 7. Decoding by Perturbation: Mitigating MLLM Hallucinations via Dynamic Textual Perturbation
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12424
- Summary: A practical, training-free inference technique for reducing multimodal hallucinations makes this work attractive for near-term deployment rather than only future model training.

### 8. SpotSound: Enhancing Large Audio-Language Models with Fine-Grained Temporal Grounding
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13023
- Summary: By improving precise event timing in audio understanding, this paper pushes audio-language models closer to real-world long-form monitoring and interaction tasks.

### 9. Multi-ORFT: Stable Online Reinforcement Fine-Tuning for Multi-Agent Diffusion Planning in Cooperative Driving
- Date: 2026-04-15
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11734
- Summary: This paper is interesting because it combines diffusion-based planning with stable online RL for cooperative driving, bridging autonomy research and modern generative planning techniques.

## Themes
- Post-training remains red-hot.
- Long-horizon agents still break in important ways.
- Multimodal reliability and hallucination control are major focuses.
- Efficient open reasoning models continue to advance.
