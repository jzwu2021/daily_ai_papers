# AI Papers Digest — 2026-04-22
Selected recent arXiv papers.

## Overview
This set of recent arXiv papers reflects a clear shift toward making advanced AI systems more measurable, controllable, and deployable. Several papers focus on better benchmarks for math, forecasting, embodied safety, and computer-use agents, while others dig into internal representations for safety and reasoning analysis. Together, they suggest that the field is increasingly concerned with reliability, real-world evaluation, and efficient deployment rather than raw capability gains alone.

## Papers
### 1. MathNet: a Global Multimodal Benchmark for Mathematical Reasoning and Retrieval
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18584
Summary: MathNet introduces a large multilingual benchmark spanning Olympiad-level problems, retrieval pairs, and retrieval-augmented solving across 47 countries and 17 languages. It matters because mathematical reasoning remains one of the clearest stress tests for advanced models, and this benchmark evaluates not just answer accuracy but whether systems can retrieve structurally relevant prior problems at global scale.

### 2. Agentic Forecasting using Sequential Bayesian Updating of Linguistic Beliefs
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18576
Summary: This paper presents BLF, an agentic forecasting system that maintains a semi-structured belief state and updates it iteratively as new evidence arrives, achieving strong results on ForecastBench. The work is important because it combines LLM tool use with explicit uncertainty tracking, making forecasting behavior more auditable and disciplined than prompt-only approaches.

### 3. LLM Safety From Within: Detecting Harmful Content with Internal Representations
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18519
Summary: The authors propose SIREN, a lightweight safety detector that uses internal model representations and safety-relevant neurons instead of relying only on terminal-layer signals. This matters because practical guardrails need to be both fast and robust, and internal-state detectors could offer a more scalable way to monitor harmful content during inference.

### 4. Using large language models for embodied planning introduces systematic safety risks
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18463
Summary: This work introduces DESPITE, a benchmark with more than 12,000 tasks for evaluating safe embodied planning, and shows that high planning performance does not automatically translate into safe behavior. The result is notable because it highlights a serious deployment gap for robotic and real-world agents: more capable systems can still produce dangerous plans with surprising frequency.

### 5. StepPO: Step-Aligned Policy Optimization for Agentic Reinforcement Learning
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18401
Summary: StepPO argues that agentic reinforcement learning should optimize at the level of action steps rather than individual tokens, better matching how real agents use tools and receive delayed rewards. This is important because coding, browser, and computer-use agents are judged on coherent multi-step behavior, so token-level RL objectives may be misaligned with the actual deployment setting.

### 6. Reasoning Models Know What's Important, and Encode It in Their Activations
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18307
Summary: The paper shows that internal activations reveal which reasoning steps are actually important to the final answer more reliably than the visible chain-of-thought text alone. It matters because it pushes interpretability toward mechanism-level signals that may be more useful for evaluating genuine reasoning than polished natural-language explanations.

### 7. MultiWorld: Scalable Multi-Agent Multi-View Video World Models
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18564
Summary: MultiWorld proposes a video world-modeling framework that supports multiple agents and synchronized viewpoints while preserving controllability over future actions and scenes. The contribution matters because realistic simulation backbones for robotics, games, and training environments need cross-view and multi-agent consistency, not just plausible videos from a single perspective.

### 8. On the Reliability of Computer Use Agents
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17849
Summary: This study examines why computer-use agents can succeed once on a task but fail when the same task is repeated, isolating stochasticity, ambiguity, and behavioral variability as core failure modes. It is significant because repeated reliability is a much stronger deployment metric than one-off success for agents expected to automate desktop and browser workflows.

### 9. GSQ: Highly-Accurate Low-Precision Scalar Quantization for LLMs via Gumbel-Softmax Sampling
Date: 2026-04-20
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18556
Summary: GSQ introduces a post-training scalar quantization method aimed at preserving more accuracy at very low bit widths without requiring overly complex inference machinery. This matters because efficient deployment remains one of the biggest constraints on local and edge LLM use, and better low-precision methods can broaden where advanced models are economically practical.

## Themes
- Evaluation is getting more realistic for math, forecasting, embodied safety, and computer-use agents.
- Researchers are using internal representations to study both harmful behavior and reasoning quality.
- Agent training objectives are shifting toward the granularity of real multi-step action rather than token prediction alone.
- World models are expanding toward multi-agent, multi-view, and deployment-oriented scenarios.
- Efficient inference remains a major priority alongside safety and capability improvements.
