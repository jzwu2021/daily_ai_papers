# AI Papers Digest — 2026-05-08
Selected recent arXiv papers.

## Overview
The newest arXiv papers reinforce a clear pattern: progress in AI is coming from better orchestration around models, not only from larger base models themselves. Recent work focuses on long-horizon agents, selective delegation, multimodal search, runtime safety, hallucination detection, and robot learning that can improve online after demonstrations. Together, these papers point toward AI systems that are more structured, more auditable, and better suited for complex real-world tasks.

## Papers
### 1. LongSeeker: Elastic Context Orchestration for Long-Horizon Search Agents
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05191
Summary: This paper introduces Context-ReAct, a framework that lets search agents actively reshape their working memory through operations such as compressing, rolling back, and deleting context. That matters because long-horizon agents often fail when their accumulated context becomes too noisy or expensive, and the reported gains on BrowseComp-style benchmarks suggest context management itself is becoming a major lever for agent reliability.

### 2. Uno-Orchestra: Parsimonious Agent Routing via Selective Delegation
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05007
Summary: The authors propose a unified orchestration policy that learns when to decompose a task and which worker or primitive should handle each subtask under a shared accuracy-cost objective. This matters because many multi-agent systems still rely on fixed routing heuristics, and a learned delegation policy could improve both performance and inference efficiency at the same time.

### 3. DecodingTrust-Agent Platform (DTap): A Controllable and Interactive Red-Teaming Platform for AI Agents
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04808
Summary: DTap presents a controllable platform for stress-testing AI agents in realistic environments where they may leak secrets, delete data, or take other harmful actions. This matters because agent security is hard to evaluate with static benchmarks alone, and a reproducible interactive testbed could make large-scale red-teaming much more systematic.

### 4. AgentTrust: Runtime Safety Evaluation and Interception for AI Agent Tool Use
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04785
Summary: This work proposes a runtime safety layer that intercepts tool calls before execution and returns verdicts such as allow, warn, block, or review. That matters because the real risk in agentic systems often appears at execution time rather than in text alone, so practical deployment needs guardrails that understand tool semantics and multi-step attack chains, not just output filtering.

### 5. The First Token Knows: Single-Decode Confidence for Hallucination Detection
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05166
Summary: The paper shows that first-token confidence from a single greedy decode can rival or beat more expensive self-consistency methods for hallucination detection in short-answer factual QA. This matters because lightweight confidence signals are easier to deploy in production than repeated decoding pipelines, making hallucination monitoring cheaper and faster.

### 6. OpenSearch-VL: An Open Recipe for Frontier Multimodal Search Agents
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05185
Summary: OpenSearch-VL offers an open-source recipe for training multimodal deep-search agents, including data synthesis pipelines and reinforcement-learning-based training. This matters because frontier multimodal search systems are usually difficult to reproduce, and a transparent recipe can help the research community study where search quality actually comes from.

### 7. From Pixels to Tokens: A Systematic Study of Latent Action Supervision for Vision-Language-Action Models
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04678
Summary: This paper compares multiple ways of supervising vision-language-action models with latent actions and finds different formulations help with different robotic capabilities. This matters because robotics models increasingly train across heterogeneous datasets, and better latent-action design could make transfer and long-horizon control more robust.

### 8. When Life Gives You BC, Make Q-functions: Extracting Q-values from Behavior Cloning for On-Robot Reinforcement Learning
Date: 2026-05-06
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05172
Summary: The authors propose Q2RL, a method that extracts Q-values from a behavior-cloned robot policy and uses them to guide online reinforcement learning without overwriting good offline behavior. This matters because offline-to-online adaptation is a central problem in practical robot learning, and the method offers a cleaner path from demonstrations to real-world improvement.

## Themes
- Long-horizon agent quality increasingly depends on memory and orchestration design, not only on base-model size.
- Safety work is moving toward runtime interception and interactive red-teaming for real tool-using agents.
- Open recipes for multimodal search and delegation could make frontier-style agent research easier to reproduce.
- Cheap confidence estimation is becoming important for production hallucination detection.
- Robotics research is focusing on how to transfer offline competence into safer and more effective online improvement.
