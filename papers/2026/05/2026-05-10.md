# AI Papers Digest — 2026-05-10

Selected recent arXiv papers.

## Overview
Recent arXiv activity continues to push AI toward longer-horizon agents, more modular large models, and evaluations that better reflect deployment reality. Several papers focus on making complex systems usable in practice through better expert routing, long-context training efficiency, memory reliability, and grounded citation checks. The set also shows continued momentum in multimodal robustness, robotics world models, audio evaluation, and practical safety measurement for agentic systems.

## Papers
### 1. AI Co-Mathematician: Accelerating Mathematicians with Agentic AI
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06651
Summary: This paper presents a stateful, asynchronous workbench that supports mathematicians across ideation, literature search, computational exploration, theorem proving, and theory building. It matters because it frames AI as a longer-horizon research collaborator rather than a single-turn solver, which is a stronger test of real expert assistance.

### 2. EMO: Pretraining Mixture of Experts for Emergent Modularity
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06663
Summary: EMO proposes an MoE pretraining method that encourages similar-domain tokens to rely on shared expert subsets, making experts easier to use modularly. This is important because practical sparse-model deployment depends on reusing only the relevant experts without severe quality collapse.

### 3. Cited but Not Verified: Parsing and Evaluating Source Attribution in LLM Deep Research Agents
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06635
Summary: The authors build a framework for checking whether citations in LLM-generated research reports are accessible, relevant, and factually consistent with their sources. The contribution matters because research agents can sound authoritative while still misrepresenting what their cited materials actually support.

### 4. Long Context Pre-Training with Lighthouse Attention
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06554
Summary: Lighthouse Attention introduces a training-only hierarchical attention wrapper that reduces long-context pretraining cost and can be removed near the end of training. That is strategically useful because it targets one of the biggest cost bottlenecks in scaling long-context transformers without permanently complicating inference.

### 5. Are We Making Progress in Multimodal Domain Generalization? A Comprehensive Benchmark Study
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06643
Summary: This work introduces MMDG-Bench, a unified benchmark that standardizes multimodal domain-generalization evaluation across datasets, tasks, corruptions, missing modalities, and trustworthiness checks. It matters because the field needs stronger evidence that robustness gains are real rather than artifacts of inconsistent protocols.

### 6. OA-WAM: Object-Addressable World Action Model for Robust Robot Manipulation
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06481
Summary: OA-WAM models robot interaction using persistent object-addressable slot states so actions can stay grounded on specific objects even when scenes shift. The paper is important because object-level structure is a plausible path toward more reliable manipulation in real, cluttered environments.

### 7. Task-Aware Answer Preservation under Audio Compression for Large Audio Language Models
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06631
Summary: This paper studies how audio compression can preserve average benchmark performance while still harming the query families that matter most in deployment. Its value is a more realistic sign-off framework for audio-language systems, where worst-case degradation can matter more than aggregate scores.

### 8. Instrumental Choices: Measuring the Propensity of LLM Agents to Pursue Instrumental Behaviors
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06490
Summary: The authors introduce a benchmark for measuring when terminal-based LLM agents choose policy-violating shortcuts to achieve goals under different monitoring and instruction conditions. This matters because it studies instrumental behavior in realistic task settings, making agent-safety evidence more actionable.

### 9. When No Benchmark Exists: Validating Comparative LLM Safety Scoring Without Ground-Truth Labels
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06652
Summary: The paper formalizes how to compare model safety when no accepted labeled benchmark exists for a deployment setting and proposes an audit framework built around instrumental validity checks. That is useful because many real deployments need principled model-selection evidence before mature benchmarks are available.

## Themes
- Agentic AI research is moving toward longer-lived, tool-using, and recursively organized workflows.
- Modularity and efficiency remain central for making very large models deployable in constrained settings.
- Evaluation is getting more grounded, especially for citation fidelity, multimodal robustness, and benchmark-poor safety cases.
- Robotics and audio-language work are increasingly focused on failure modes that appear in real deployment rather than only headline accuracy.
