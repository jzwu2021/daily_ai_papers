# AI Papers Digest — 2026-05-16

Selected recent arXiv papers.

## Overview
Recent arXiv papers are converging on the operational problems that appear when agentic and multimodal systems leave the lab. The strongest work from May 14 focuses on long-horizon adaptation, test-time reasoning, retrieval design, modular memory, embodied unification, and better evaluation for video and agents. Together these papers suggest the field is putting more attention on reliability, efficiency, and sustained performance under real deployment constraints.

## Papers
### 1. FutureSim: Replaying World Events to Evaluate Adaptive Agents
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15188
Summary: FutureSim evaluates agents by replaying real news and question-resolution streams in chronological order, asking them to adapt beyond their knowledge cutoff while forecasting world events. It matters because the benchmark targets long-horizon adaptation under realistic information flow, and the paper shows even frontier agents remain weak in this setting.

### 2. OpenDeepThink: Parallel Reasoning via Bradley--Terry Aggregation
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15177
Summary: OpenDeepThink scales reasoning breadth by sampling multiple candidate solutions and ranking them with pairwise Bradley-Terry aggregation instead of relying on a single chain of thought. The contribution is important because it offers a practical path to test-time compute scaling, with large gains on objectively verifiable tasks such as Codeforces-style problem solving.

### 3. MeMo: Memory as a Model
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15156
Summary: MeMo stores newly acquired knowledge in a dedicated memory model while leaving the base LLM unchanged, enabling updates without catastrophic forgetting or access to model weights. This is useful because it offers a plug-and-play way to keep both open and closed models current while holding inference cost independent of corpus size.

### 4. Is Grep All You Need? How Agent Harnesses Reshape Agentic Search
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15184
Summary: This paper compares grep-based and vector-based retrieval across multiple agent harnesses and tool-calling styles for long-context search tasks. Its value is highly practical: retrieval quality depends not just on the retriever, but also on how tools are wrapped and how their outputs are presented inside agent loops.

### 5. Self-Distilled Agentic Reinforcement Learning
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15155
Summary: SDAR combines reinforcement learning with a gated self-distillation objective that strengthens supervision on teacher-endorsed tokens while softening unreliable negative guidance. The result matters because long-horizon agents often receive sparse reward signals, so better dense supervision can translate into more stable and effective post-training.

### 6. ATLAS: Agentic or Latent Visual Reasoning? One Word is Enough for Both
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15198
Summary: ATLAS introduces functional tokens that act both as latent reasoning units and as agentic operations, aiming to combine the strengths of tool-using and latent visual reasoning approaches. It is significant because it proposes a scalable way to improve visual reasoning without requiring expensive explicit visual intermediate generation or custom architectures.

### 7. Pelican-Unified 1.0: A Unified Embodied Intelligence Model for Understanding, Reasoning, Imagination and Action
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15153
Summary: Pelican-Unified 1.0 uses a single unified model to handle scene understanding, reasoning, future imagination, and action generation for embodied tasks. This is notable because robotics and embodied AI have often treated these capabilities as separate systems, while this paper argues that joint optimization can preserve strength across all of them.

### 8. EntityBench: Towards Entity-Consistent Long-Range Multi-Shot Video Generation
Date: 2026-05-14
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15199
Summary: EntityBench introduces a benchmark for long-range multi-shot video generation with explicit tracking of characters, objects, and locations across many shots and recurrence gaps. It matters because long-form video generation needs stronger evaluation of cross-shot consistency, and the paper shows current methods still degrade sharply as narratives get longer.

## Themes
- Agent evaluation is moving toward realistic, dynamic settings that require adaptation over time instead of one-shot benchmark answers.
- Test-time compute, search design, and memory architecture are becoming central levers for improving agent performance.
- Multimodal reasoning research is increasingly focused on efficient intermediate representations rather than only larger generators.
- Embodied AI is pushing toward unified models that connect understanding, planning, imagination, and action.
- Better benchmarks are emerging for long-horizon failure modes, especially in agents and video generation.
