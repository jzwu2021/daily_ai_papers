# AI Papers Digest — 2026-05-11

Selected recent arXiv papers.

## Overview
Recent arXiv activity remains heavily focused on making agentic and multimodal systems more usable in realistic settings. The most interesting papers span long-horizon delegation, reusable skill learning, modular sparse models, long-context efficiency, grounded citation checking, and object-centric robotics. Taken together, they suggest the field is shifting from raw capability demos toward systems that can scale, verify themselves better, and stay reliable under real deployment constraints.

## Papers
### 1. AI Co-Mathematician: Accelerating Mathematicians with Agentic AI
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06651
Summary: This paper presents a stateful, asynchronous workbench that supports mathematicians across ideation, literature search, computational exploration, theorem proving, and theory building. It matters because it frames AI as a longer-horizon research collaborator rather than a single-turn solver, which is a stronger test of real expert assistance.

### 2. Recursive Agent Optimization
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06639
Summary: The authors propose a reinforcement-learning approach for recursive agents that can spawn and delegate subtasks to new instances of themselves. This is important because recursive delegation offers a concrete path to inference-time scaling on problems that exceed a single model’s context window.

### 3. SkillOS: Learning Skill Curation for Self-Evolving Agents
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06614
Summary: SkillOS studies how agents can curate reusable skills from experience instead of solving each task from scratch, using RL to learn longer-term curation policies. The contribution matters because durable skill reuse is one of the clearest ways to make agent systems improve over time instead of remaining one-off wrappers around LLMs.

### 4. EMO: Pretraining Mixture of Experts for Emergent Modularity
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06663
Summary: EMO proposes an MoE pretraining method that encourages similar-domain tokens to rely on shared expert subsets, making experts more independently reusable. This is strategically useful because practical sparse-model deployment depends on modularity and selective activation, especially in memory-constrained environments.

### 5. Long Context Pre-Training with Lighthouse Attention
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06554
Summary: Lighthouse Attention introduces a training-only hierarchical attention wrapper that reduces the cost of very long-context pretraining and can be removed toward the end of training. That is valuable because long-context capability is increasingly important, but current training costs remain one of the biggest scaling bottlenecks.

### 6. Cited but Not Verified: Parsing and Evaluating Source Attribution in LLM Deep Research Agents
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06635
Summary: The paper presents a framework for checking whether citations in LLM-generated research reports are accessible, relevant, and factually consistent with the cited sources. This matters because research agents can sound authoritative while still misrepresenting what their references actually support.

### 7. Are We Making Progress in Multimodal Domain Generalization? A Comprehensive Benchmark Study
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06643
Summary: This work introduces a unified benchmark for multimodal domain generalization across datasets, corruptions, missing modalities, and trustworthiness checks. It is important because multimodal robustness claims are often difficult to compare when evaluation protocols vary widely across papers.

### 8. OA-WAM: Object-Addressable World Action Model for Robust Robot Manipulation
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06481
Summary: OA-WAM models robot interaction with persistent object-addressable slot states so actions can stay grounded on specific objects even when scenes shift. The approach is promising because object-level structure may be a practical route to more reliable manipulation in cluttered real environments.

### 9. Task-Aware Answer Preservation under Audio Compression for Large Audio Language Models
Date: 2026-05-07
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06631
Summary: This paper studies when audio compression preserves average benchmark performance while still damaging the query families that matter most in deployment. Its value is a more realistic sign-off framework for audio-language systems, where worst-case degradation can matter more than headline average scores.

## Themes
- Agent research is moving toward longer-lived systems that delegate, accumulate skills, and improve from experience.
- Efficiency work is focusing on the training bottlenecks that block modular sparse models and long-context transformers.
- Evaluation is getting more grounded, especially for citation fidelity, multimodal robustness, and deployment-specific failure modes.
- Robotics and audio-language papers are increasingly targeting the kinds of brittleness that appear in real-world use.
