# AI Papers Digest — 2026-04-15

Selected recent arXiv papers.

1. 2026-04-15
Self-Distillation Zero: Self-Revision Turns Binary Rewards into Dense Supervision
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12002
Why it matters: Promising post-training idea that converts sparse binary rewards into denser supervision, potentially improving reasoning efficiency in math/code tasks.

2. 2026-04-15
Nemotron 3 Super: Open, Efficient Mixture-of-Experts Hybrid Mamba-Transformer Model for Agentic Reasoning
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12374
Why it matters: Big open-model release focused on strong reasoning plus better inference efficiency and long context.

3. 2026-04-15
Relax: An Asynchronous Reinforcement Learning Engine for Omni-Modal Post-Training at Scale
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11554
Why it matters: Important systems paper for scaling RL post-training across text, image, audio, video, and agents.

4. 2026-04-15
Filtered Reasoning Score: Evaluating Reasoning Quality on a Model's Most-Confident Traces
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11996
Why it matters: Better reasoning evaluation is a bottleneck right now; this proposes a metric beyond raw final-answer accuracy.

5. 2026-04-15
The Long-Horizon Task Mirage? Diagnosing Where and Why Agentic Systems Break
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11978
Why it matters: Long-horizon reliability is one of the biggest unsolved agent problems, so this benchmark/diagnostic work is especially timely.

6. 2026-04-15
Benchmarking Deflection and Hallucination in Large Vision-Language Models
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12033
Why it matters: Directly targets whether multimodal models know when not to answer, which is crucial for safe deployment.

7. 2026-04-15
Decoding by Perturbation: Mitigating MLLM Hallucinations via Dynamic Textual Perturbation
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12424
Why it matters: A training-free inference technique for reducing multimodal hallucination is practical and likely to get attention.

8. 2026-04-15
SpotSound: Enhancing Large Audio-Language Models with Fine-Grained Temporal Grounding
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13023
Why it matters: Moves audio-language models toward precise event timing instead of coarse clip-level understanding.

9. 2026-04-15
Multi-ORFT: Stable Online Reinforcement Fine-Tuning for Multi-Agent Diffusion Planning in Cooperative Driving
https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11734
Why it matters: Interesting crossover of diffusion planning and online RL for autonomous driving and multi-agent coordination.

Themes
- Post-training remains red-hot.
- Long-horizon agents still break in important ways.
- Multimodal reliability and hallucination control are major focuses.
- Efficient open reasoning models continue to advance.
